PFD Experience Blog

Justin Chen

8 February, 2023

Before the module began, we were given a set of problem statements ranging from digital solutions from OCBC to social entrepreneurship in Ngee Ann Polytechnic. With my level of competency in terms of programming, I chose the problem statement which seemed to me has the least coding involved.

In the first week of my Portfolio Development (PFD) module, I came into the class with absolutely no expectations or whatsoever as we were only given a problem statement. I thought to myself that maybe this is another design thinking module similar to Innovation Made Possible (IMP) which is to utilize design thinking and create a prototype without any coding involved. However, after the first lesson I was taken aback by what this module is really about. We were tasked to implement full stack development in this module in response to the problem statement given. The problem statement I chose to work on was about sustainability at Ngee Ann Polytechnic. In the second lesson, an activity was conducted to determine how our groups were formed which was done by the selection of people's strength and weakness such that everyone has a chance to work with someone they do not know but through their abilities. My group consisted of 5 people from different classes. We introduced ourselves and stated our strengths to one another, and with that concludes the first week of PFD.

In the second week, both lessons were spent ideating on a solution based on our given problem statement. Communications was definitely lacking in these lessons as we were slowly trying to get acquainted with each other. As such, individually we researched on the types of sustainability there are to gain a better understanding to the problem statement. At this point, I started to feel worried based on what kind of product we eventually have to create. This is because, in the first week, we shared our strengths and weaknesses, and our entire group is not competent in programming at all. Hence, I started to feel that creating a solution with full stack development will be a daunting task.

In the third and fourth week, we started having a rough idea on the prototype we aim in creating which was to create an informative website for our chosen sustainability category. Throughout these weeks, we encountered various of hiccups along the way. Initially, we chose e-waste sustainability as it is not a concept that is being touched on as concise as other types of sustainability in Ngee Ann Polytechnic. We first thought of an idea which is to hold an “E-waste Fiesta” week where students and staffs can bring their electronic waste to school and donate it. However, it came with problems like “How does it answer the problem statement since no technology was used?” and “Even if we held this fiesta, students will not be encouraged because there are no incentives. Even if there is an incentive, where can we get the funding from?”. We were stuck on ideating a solution where funding is not an issue and how we can implement technology (full stack development) into it. As a result, we decided to take a 180 degree turn and change our direction to food sustainability. We consulted with our lecturer, Dr Joel Yang, and he advised us to stick to e-waste sustainability because it is an interesting topic that is both a current and important issue in today’s society. As such, we decided to stick to e-waste sustainability and work our way from there. We had an opportunity to visit Ngee Ann Polytechnic’s Integrated Operations Centre (IOC) which was truly one of the most interesting parts in this module. A mini tour was conducted by Mr Charles Keck where he showed us how the IOC was operated, ranging from how campus security is implemented, electricity is monitored and what was being done to work towards a “Green Campus”. The tour made me realised how wonderful and important technology is in tackling multiple global issues and also aid in advocating a sustainable lifestyle to everyone. Eventually, we settled on addressing the issue of e-waste sustainability in Ngee Ann Polytechnic. Our group finalized on an idea which was to create an informative website that has functionalities similar to Carousell. We decided to start working on our website early to get the necessary requirements ready for the hackathon in week 5.

The day of the Hackathon finally arrived. I was having mixed feelings about it because this is my first ever Hackathon experience and I was not sure what to expect from this. We started off the first day of the Hackathon with an introductory speech by one of the Deputy Directors of the school and mentioned the various activities and food provided throughout the Hackathon. After which we dissipated to our respective rooms to work on our product. From around 9am all the way to 6pm, my group and I was working on our prototype, making sure we were able to implement our planned functionalities onto our website. It was really a very tough and energy-draining process as we encountered various of problems code wise that we had to troubleshoot. It was also mainly because one of our group members was unable to attend the Hackathon, leading us to take on her part of the work which made it even more stressful than it already is. Thankfully, the snacks and drinks provided definitely served as an energy replenisher for us.

Undoubtedly, the first day of the Hackathon was filled with large amount of frustration and tiredness. However, coming into the second day, things were much better as I now know what to expect from it. Similar to the first day, our group worked tirelessly to make sure we implement our planned features onto our website regardless of the challenges faced. It was for sure much more smooth sailing as compared to the first day. I still remember as soon as the time hit 5.45pm, that was the time I led out a sigh of relief that not only has the torturous journey has ended, but we also managed to finish our prototype. It was definitely a sight to see the smiles on my teammates’ faces as our hard work has finally come into fruition.

Coming into the final day of the Hackathon which is our presentation. Despite being confident in showcasing our work, I have to admit that I was definitely not very proud of it simply because it did not look aesthetically pleasing, and our functionalities were certainly lacking. However, I was still happy to present it to the judges and the class to show the amount of hard work and time put into this. After our presentation, the judges, Dr Joel Yang and Mr Charles Keck complimented on our idea and gave us improvements to work on for our prototype. This was when I felt a heavy weight lifted up from my shoulders that I am finally free from this Hackathon. We ended the Hackathon with a closing speech by Dr Joel Yang as well as a Lucky Draw.

Without a doubt, there are much to be improved on for our website in which we intend to incorporate the judges comments, as well as other good-to-have features that are commonly found in these type of websites. Until Term 2 starts, I went on a break having to forget the entire module completely.

After the new years, term 2 started which commences the start of PFD module again. Honestly, it was hard to kick start my engine again and have the motivation to continue improving on our prototype, but because I was the unanimously decided group leader, I had to lead my team to complete the various improvements we were told and wanted to do by the end of the assignment.

The beginning of week 12 was very dreadful, not only was there multiple assignments starting for other modules as well, but the thought of improving our website isn't exactly the most exciting thing. To start things off, I decided that it was time for a better UI/UX design to our website as it currently lacks both professionalism and aestheticism. After researching on various similiar websites and inspirations, I have finally found a design that is simple yet aesthetically pleasing which was what we ended up using for our final prototype. In addition to this, I also revamped our sign in and sign up page which was atrociously designed previously as I lacked the time management during the Hackathon. That practically summed up the entire experience of week 12 for PFD.

Coming into week 13-16, this was it felt like I am reliving the times from the Hackathon. In week 13, after redesigning the entire website, I wanted to test the uploading of products into our "Marketplace". All of a sudden, the items that was uploaded could not be retrieved from the database and displayed onto our "Marketplace" page. This caused me to immediately panicked as this was one of the most important feature of our prototype and if it does not work, our prototype will literally be rendered as useless. At that time, everyone else was busy with their assignments and was not able to help in fixing this error that I came across. Seeing that I was not the one who coded this functionality, it took me a long and torturous 4 hours to resolve this issue. What made it worse was that the error all lied in one line of code which happened to be a logic flaw. However, all of this is part of the experience as a person in the field of IT.

The stress for me was definitely piling up in week 13 and 14 as not only do I have to handle countless of assignments I had from other modules, but I have to also think of useful functionalities that will be useful for our website and code them out. In all honesty, it is very obvious that everyone hated PFD module for various of reasons, mainly is the way group formation was done where everyone would pick people whose strengths are good at coding which lead to an eventual imbalance of coding abilities amongst groups. This was perhaps why I would say my group was not particulaly motivated in this assignment as well as our grade for this module does not transalate to the amount of hard work put into it. Hence, I started getting stressed about the completion of the prototype as at that point I was the only one trying to salvage from our situation to at least showcase a better product as before.

On the bright side, I managed to complete various of functionalities both on the front-end and back-end through week 13-16. There was definitely countless of ups and downs along the way, ranging from the happiness of successfully incorporating a workable functionality to the website, to the endless amount of errors implementing my functionalities. On top of my own contributions, I had to fix my other groupmates codes and errors which was certainly not enjoyable but was a fulfilling experience that for once, as someone who is not particularly incline in the technical aspect, they would rely on me to fix their errors. To say the least, this entire experience was not a total disappointment at all. It was an overall very enriching and fulfilling experience as it certainly tested my mental fortitude throughout the Hackathon. Not only am I thankful for the opportunity Ngee Ann Polytechnic has given me, but I am also grateful for my 3 other teammates who were with me on this rigorous journey. Despite us all being from different classes and only trying to get acquainted with each other twice every week, our bonds were certainly strengthened during the Hackathon and needless to say, at the end of the module. We complemented each other really well as we leveraged on each other strengths and convert our weaknesses into motivation and commitment such that we were able to overcome the myriad of obstacles faced. I am really glad we resolved any impediments without conflicts and managed to produce a prototype we were all really satisfied with. If there was another opportunity for me to partake in another experience similar to this, I will definitely sign up for it without any hesitation.

Most importantly, a huge amount of gratitude and appreciation have to be shown towards my lecturer, Dr Joel Yang, who was someone that went the extra mile as not just being our lecturer, but certainly the strongest pillar of support for the entirety of this module. A huge thanks is also needed to be said to my groupmates, Adam, Keanan and Jia Yuan for doing their best and executing their responsibilites to eventually create this website that we are all proud of. Needless to say, this will definitely be a core memory in Ngee Ann Polytechnic.
